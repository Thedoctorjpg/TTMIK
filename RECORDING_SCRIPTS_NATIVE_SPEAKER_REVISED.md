### Track 03: Style_Overview_Grammar_Casual_Slang_Formal.mp3
**Duration:** 8 minutes

**(0:00-1:00) FOUR-STYLE SYSTEM OVERVIEW**
"Korean has four main speaking styles: Formal, Polite, Casual, and Slang. Understanding when to use each shows cultural intelligence."

**(1:00-2:00) FORMAL STYLE (하십시오체)**
"Formal style is used with elders, in business, or public announcements. 
Example: '안녕하십니까?' (An-nyeong-ha-sim-ni-kka?) - formal hello
Use when: Business meetings, speaking to elders, formal announcements"

**(2:00-3:00) POLITE STYLE (해요체)**
"Polite style is most common in daily life, showing respect without formality.
Example: '안녕하세요?' (An-nyeong-ha-se-yo?) - polite hello
Use when: General conversation, customer service, initial meetings"

**(3:00-4:00) CASUAL STYLE (해체)**
"Casual style is used with friends, family, or peers. This is what modern young Koreans use daily.
Example: '안녕!' (An-nyeong!) - casual hello
Use when: Friends, peers, age-similar people, social media"

**(4:00-5:00) SLANG/ABBREVIATED STYLE**
"Slang and abbreviated expressions are common among younger Koreans. These change rapidly with culture.
Example: '아아' (A-a) - iced Americanico (Starbucks abbreviation)
Use when: Young friends, casual settings, understanding modern culture"

**(5:00-6:00) NATURAL KOREAN SPEECH**
"In modern Korea, especially among young people, particle drops are natural:
Full: '이것은 얼마입니까?' (This is how much?)
Natural: '이거 얼마예요?' (This how much?) - dropped '이것은'
Most casual: '이거 얼마?' (This how much?) - even more dropped"

**(6:00-7:00) STYLE-SWITCHING PRACTICE**
"Practice switching between styles based on context. With elders: formal/polite. With peers: casual. In business: formal. In social media: slang."

**(7:00-8:00) CLOSING**
"Your style-switching ability shows cultural intelligence. Don't stress about perfection. Context awareness matters more than perfect grammar."

### Track 04: Pronunciation_Basics.mp3
**Duration:** 6 minutes

**(0:00-1:00) KOREAN SOUNDS**
"Korean has some sounds that don't exist in English. The key is listening and practicing, not perfecting immediately."

**(1:00-2:00) KEY SOUNDS**
"'ㅈ' (j) - softer than English J, like 'jeong'
'ㅊ' (ch) - softer than English CH, like 'cheong'  
'ㄹ' (r/l) - between R and L, like 'hangeul'
'ㅂ' (p/b) - at start of word is 'p', in middle is 'b'"

**(2:00-3:00) RHYTHM AND INTONATION**
"Korean has different rhythm than English. Each syllable has equal weight. Intonation rises at questions, falls at statements."

**(3:00-4:00) LINKING SOUNDS**
"Korean sounds blend together naturally. Don't overly separate sounds. Let them flow naturally as you would in your native language."

**(4:00-5:00) DON'T STRESS PERFECTION**
"Native Koreans can understand imperfect pronunciation. Your effort is valued over accuracy. Clear communication matters more than perfect sounds."

**(5:00-6:00) PRACTICE ENCOURAGEMENT**
"Listen to Korean media naturally. Practice phrases without stress. Your pronunciation will improve naturally through use, not through perfectionism."
