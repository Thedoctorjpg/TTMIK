# The Hollow Verdict
### A Solo D&D 5e Mystery Campaign — High Fantasy
**Starting Level:** 3 | **Estimated End Level:** 6-7
**Party:** Korr Vandersteen (Goliath Cleric, Knowledge Domain) + Pip Ashgrove (Halfling Rogue, Inquisitive)

---

## Premise

The city of **Greyhollow Reach** sits at the crossing point of three rival nations — Velmora (a militarized monarchy), the Ashfen Compact (a merchant oligarchy), and the Thornward Circle (a druidic confederation). For two centuries, the city has remained neutral, governed by the **Verdict Court**, a council of magical judges bound to the **Hollow Bell** — an artifact that compels absolute truth from anyone who speaks before it.

Three nights ago, **Arbiter Velence Mar**, the Lead Arbiter of the Court, was found dead in the Bell Chamber — a sealed vault accessible only to the seven Arbiters. No forced entry. No struggle. And critically: **the Hollow Bell did not ring**, which it does automatically whenever a lie is spoken or violence occurs within 30 feet of it. By every magical measure, nothing happened in that room.

The three factions are each one accusation away from declaring the treaty broken. The remaining Arbiters, desperate for a neutral party with no political stake, have hired Korr and Pip — outsiders, recently arrived in the city — to investigate quietly before any faction moves first.

**Deadline:** The Accord Renewal Ceremony is in **7 in-game days**. If the killer isn't found (or a scapegoat isn't found first by someone else), the ceremony becomes a flashpoint.

---

## The Truth (GM Eyes Only — Do Not Reveal Early)

Arbiter Velence Mar was killed by **Arbiter Cassia Thorne**, the second-most senior judge — but not through violence or lies in the traditional sense.

Velence had discovered that the Hollow Bell's truth-compulsion was failing — not broken, but being **slowly drained** by a ritual Cassia had been running for eleven years, secretly siphoning a fraction of the Bell's power to maintain a *masking enchantment* on herself. Cassia is not what she appears: she is **Veyne Ashcroft**, a Velmoran spy who infiltrated the Court decades ago and has been quietly tilting "neutral" rulings toward Velmoran interests ever since.

Velence confronted her privately. Cassia didn't lie to him (which would have triggered the Bell) — she instead used a rare ritual dagger, **the Quietus Needle**, which kills painlessly and instantly, with a side effect: it also erases the truth-compulsion's *record* of the last 10 minutes in the chamber, making it seem as though nothing occurred. The Bell didn't "ring" because, magically speaking, *as far as the Bell's memory is concerned, nothing happened.*

Cassia's goal now: ensure the investigation either stalls, gets pinned on a faction (triggering the crisis she can exploit), or gets pinned on an innocent scapegoat — **Arbiter Toman Vask** (Ashfen-aligned), whom she's quietly framing.

## Suspects

### 1. Arbiter Cassia Thorne (TRUE CULPRIT)
- **Role:** Second-most senior Arbiter, "neutral" elder stateswoman, beloved by the public
- **Motive (apparent):** None — she has the most to lose from instability
- **Motive (real):** Protecting her decades-long identity as a Velmoran agent
- **Tells:** Too helpful, too available, subtly redirects suspicion toward Vask. Detect Magic on her reveals nothing (the masking enchantment is keyed to resist it) — but a Religion or Arcana check (DC 17) on the *residual energy in the Bell Chamber* reveals "a drain pattern inconsistent with any known ritual currently active in the city," which is the first thread that leads to her over multiple sessions.
- **If confronted directly too early:** She will calmly, "truthfully" deflect — she's skilled at technically-true statements that mislead. The Bell won't catch her because she never outright lies.

### 2. Arbiter Toman Vask (FRAMED)
- **Role:** Ashfen-aligned Arbiter, ambitious, abrasive
- **Motive:** Publicly argued with Velence Mar two days before the murder over a trade ruling
- **Evidence planted against him:** A torn page from his personal ledger found near the Bell Chamber (Cassia planted it), and a servant (bribed by Cassia) who claims to have overheard him threaten Velence
- **Truth:** Innocent, but arrogant enough that he initially lies about *unrelated* things (an affair, financial troubles) when questioned — which DOES trigger normal lie-detection (Insight checks, Zone of Truth), making him look far guiltier than he is. This is the campaign's central red herring.

### 3. Brother Hask (Temple Groundskeeper)
- **Role:** Quiet, elderly groundskeeper of the Court's attached temple, tends the Bell Chamber's approach
- **Motive:** None apparent
- **Relevance:** He's the only person who saw Cassia enter the corridor near the chamber that night — but he's nearly blind and initially describes "a tall figure in Arbiter robes," which could be any of the seven. Talking to him repeatedly, with patience (not interrogation), eventually yields a detail: the figure "smelled like cold iron and lavender" — Cassia's signature scent, used to mask the smell of the masking enchantment's reagents. This is a slow-burn clue, not a quick win.

### 4. The Velmoran Trade Envoy, Ser Dalric Voss
- **Role:** Official Velmoran representative in the city, openly ambitious about Velmoran influence
- **Motive (apparent):** Obvious — a destabilized Court benefits Velmora
- **Truth:** He's a genuine red herring with no connection to Cassia at all (he doesn't know her true identity either). He's loud, suspicious-acting, and easy to dislike, but ultimately just a political operator doing politics. Useful for misdirection and for a tense roleplay/social encounter.

## Key Locations

### The Verdict Court (Hub Location)
- **Bell Chamber:** Sealed crime scene. Investigation DC 15 finds the Quietus Needle's faint puncture wound on the body (easy to miss — Medicine check DC 16, or DC 12 if Korr specifically examines for "a wound that wouldn't have caused noise or struggle"). Arcana/Religion DC 17 finds the drain-pattern clue.
- **Arbiters' Private Chambers:** Each of the seven Arbiters has quarters here. Cassia's quarters (if searched — requires stealth/distraction, since she's rarely away) contain a hidden compartment (Investigation DC 18) with a single object: an old Velmoran military signet ring, decades out of fashion. This is the campaign's smoking gun, but it requires real effort and risk to find.
- **The Hollow Bell itself:** Can be consulted (with permission) — anyone may speak before it once per day and have a single statement verified. Smart players will realize they can use this on suspects directly. Toman Vask will agree and pass (on the murder question) but fail on unrelated lies. Cassia will skillfully avoid ever being asked a direct yes/no question about the murder, deflecting any attempt to arrange it ("the Bell's power is fragile right now, given recent events — surely you understand we must be careful with it").

### The Lantern District (Investigation/Social Hub)
- Greyhollow's merchant quarter. Where Brother Hask can be found at a tea house most evenings. Also where Ser Dalric Voss can be encountered and questioned (or fought, if things go badly).
- **Encounter:** A break-in attempt at Korr and Pip's lodgings on Night 2 — Cassia, realizing the investigators are asking the right kinds of questions about "drain patterns," sends two hired thugs (not to kill, but to steal/destroy any notes). This is the first combat encounter.

### The Temple Archives (Exploration/Dungeon-style)
- Beneath the Court, a maze of old record-vaults. Contains historical records of the Bell's enchantment — comparing old maintenance logs to current readings is how players can independently arrive at "something has been draining this Bell for over a decade," narrowing the suspect pool to long-tenured Arbiters (only Cassia and one retired Arbiter fit; the retired one is easily cleared, leaving Cassia).
- **Encounter:** Animated guardian constructs (2x Animated Armor) protect the deepest archive room — not hostile unless the party tries to remove records, only engages if they attempt to leave with originals rather than copying them.

### Cassia's Safehouse (Climax Location)
- A modest house in the Lantern District under a false name. Found only via the signet ring clue (tracing Velmoran military registries) or by following Cassia herself (Stealth contest).
- Contains: ritual components for the masking enchantment, correspondence in Velmoran military cipher, and — if the party arrives at the right time — Cassia herself, attempting to destroy the evidence.

## Clue Tracker

Use this as a checklist. Players don't need all of these — three or four solid threads are enough to confront Cassia, but more clues make the final confrontation easier (she has fewer technically-true deflections available).

| # | Clue | Where Found | Points To |
|---|------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Quietus Needle wound on body | Bell Chamber (Medicine DC 16) | Murder weapon is magical, painless, silent |
| 2 | Drain-pattern in residual Bell energy | Bell Chamber (Arcana/Religion DC 17) | Long-term ritual draining the Bell |
| 3 | Torn ledger page (planted) | Near Bell Chamber (Investigation DC 12) | Points to Vask (red herring) |
| 4 | Bribed servant's testimony | Lantern District, social encounter | Points to Vask (red herring — collapses under Insight/Zone of Truth) |
| 5 | "Cold iron and lavender" scent description | Brother Hask, requires 2-3 patient conversations | Identifies a specific Arbiter's signature scent |
| 6 | Old maintenance logs vs. current drain rate | Temple Archives | Narrows suspects to long-tenured Arbiters |
| 7 | Velmoran signet ring | Cassia's quarters (Investigation DC 18, high risk) | Direct link to Velmoran identity |
| 8 | Quietus Needle origin (a rare Velmoran assassin's tool) | Can be researched via Archives or a black-market contact in Lantern District | Links weapon type to Velmora |

**Minimum to act:** Clues 1, 2, and 5 or 7 together are enough for the party to reasonably accuse Cassia and trigger the climax. Fewer than that, and accusing her publicly will fail (the Court won't act on a hunch against their most respected member) — the party would need to either get more proof or set a trap.

## Encounters Summary

1. **Night 2 — Break-in at lodgings.** 2x Bandit (or Thug, MM stats) sent by Cassia. Non-lethal goal (steal notes), but will fight if cornered. CR-appropriate for level 3.
2. **Temple Archives — Animated Guardians.** 2x Animated Armor. Avoidable with care (copying records instead of removing them); a real fight if triggered. CR-appropriate for level 3-4.
3. **Optional — Ser Dalric Voss confrontation.** If players accuse him publicly without evidence, this becomes a social/legal complication rather than combat — but if they break into his quarters, his Velmoran guard detail (2-3 Veteran-lite or Guard stats) will engage.
4. **Climax — Cassia's Safehouse.** Cassia (use a modified Cult Fanatic or Mage statblock, with the Quietus Needle as a special silent sneak-attack weapon, plus 1-2 hired Velmoran agents). She will attempt to flee or destroy evidence before fighting; cornering her is part of the challenge. CR-appropriate for level 6-7 (this is the finale, designed to be tough).

## Possible Outcomes / Endings

- **Full Success:** Cassia exposed and captured/killed with solid evidence before the Accord Renewal. Vask is cleared, the treaty holds, and the party gains significant reputation in Greyhollow Reach as neutral problem-solvers — a strong hook for future adventures.
- **Partial Success:** Cassia is stopped, but without enough proof to reveal her Velmoran ties publicly — the Court quietly "retires" her, and Velmora's role is never exposed. The treaty holds, but Velmora remains an unaddressed threat for future campaigns.
- **Failure:** If the deadline passes without resolution, Vask is publicly accused (based on the planted evidence) and the Ashfen Compact withdraws from the treaty in protest, fracturing the Accord. Cassia escapes and remains a recurring antagonist. The city slides toward open conflict — a darker campaign continuation.

## GM Notes

- **Pacing:** This is built for roughly 5-8 sessions. Sessions 1-2: Bell Chamber investigation + first red herrings (Vask). Sessions 3-4: Lantern District, break-in, Brother Hask's slow clue. Sessions 5-6: Temple Archives, narrowing suspects. Session 7-8: Confronting Cassia, climax.
- **Cassia as a recurring "friendly" NPC:** Throughout the investigation, Cassia should appear *helpful* — offering resources, vouching for the party, even subtly steering them toward (false) leads in ways that feel like good-faith assistance. The reveal lands harder if players liked her.
- **Korr's Zone of Truth:** Tempting to use early and often. If cast directly on Cassia, she simply won't answer incriminating questions ("I decline to discuss this matter further" is not a lie). This should feel frustrating in a "this is clearly suspicious but not technically illegal" way — a clue in itself.
- **Pip's role:** Best used for breaking into Cassia's quarters, tailing suspects, and reading social cues the bigger, more conspicuous Korr can't.
